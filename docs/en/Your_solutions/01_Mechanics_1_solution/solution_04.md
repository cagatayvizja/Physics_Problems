1. Projectile MotionThe Concept: When an object is fired at an angle, it moves in two independent directions. Horizontally, it moves at a constant speed ($a_x = 0$). Vertically, it is slowed down and then sped up by gravity ($a_y = -g$).Differential Equations: $\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} = 0$ and $\frac{d^2y}{dt^2} = -g$.Key Results:Time of Flight: The total time in the air is 12.2 seconds.Max Height: The peak of the trajectory is 184 meters.Range: The total horizontal distance traveled is 979 meters.2. Range OptimizationThe Concept: To find the maximum distance (Range), we look at the formula $R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$.The Logic: The sine function is at its maximum when the angle is $90^\circ$. Therefore, $2\theta = 90^\circ$, which means $\theta = \mathbf{45^\circ}$. This is the ideal launch angle for maximum distance.3. Path IntersectionThe Concept: Two objects move along different mathematical paths. We check if they reach the same $(x, y)$ coordinates at the exact same time ($t$).The Result: While their paths physically cross on a map, Alice and Bob arrive at that crossing point at different times. Therefore, they do not collide.4. Vector CalculusThe Concept: Motion is hierarchical.Velocity is the first derivative of position (how fast position changes).Acceleration is the second derivative of position (how fast velocity changes).The Logic: By differentiating $r(t)$, we find the specific vector formulas for how the object speeds up and turns.5. Relative VelocityThe Concept: To cross a river directly (North) while the current flows East, the boat must aim slightly upstream (West) to cancel out the river's push.The Result: The boat should head at an angle of 23.6° West of North. It will take 43.6 seconds to cross.6. Variable VelocityThe Concept: If velocity is a function of time ($t^2 + 2t - 5$), the object is not moving at a constant speed.The Logic: We use integration to find the position and differentiation to find the acceleration. At $t=3$, the acceleration is 8 m/s².7. Elimination of TimeThe Concept: By removing the "time" variable ($t$), we can see the geometric shape of the path on a graph ($y$ vs $x$).The Observation: The acceleration is not a single number; it changes as time passes ($a$ is a function of $t$). Therefore, the acceleration is not constant.8. Circular MotionThe Concept: Even standing still at the Equator, you are moving in a circle due to Earth's rotation. This creates centripetal acceleration.The Result: This acceleration is very small (0.034 m/s²), which is why we don't feel like we are being "flung" off the planet; gravity is much stronger.9. Momentum ComparisonThe Concept: Momentum is the "strength" of a moving object ($p = mv$).The Winner: Even though a fly is faster, its mass is tiny.
